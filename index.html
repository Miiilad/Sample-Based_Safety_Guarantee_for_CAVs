Sample-Based Safety Guarantee in Distributed Heterogeneous Vehicular Platooning for Deep Neural Network-Based Control

Milad Farsi1, Shuhao Bian,1, Nasser L. Azad1, Chris Hobbs2,
1Automation and Intelligent Systems Group, University of Waterloo, 2BlackBerry QNX

Abstract

This paper presents a novel framework to facilitate coordination and guarantee safety in distributed heterogeneous platooning systems using a learning-based approach. Motivated by the growing need to ensure the safety and reliability of learning-based platooning systems, which are increasingly being considered for autonomous transportation in complex, uncertain, and adversarial environments, we obtain sample-based safety guarantees utilizing Control Barrier Functions (CBFs). This is integrated with a Model Predictive Control (MPC) scheme tailored to homogenize dynamics through Deep Neural Network (DNN) models and coordinate vehicles in a heterogeneous platoon. In this mechanism, a High-Order Control Barrier Function (HOCBF) is obtained using samples and implemented within each vehicle that acts as a shield to ensure safe operation even under uncertain conditions arising from DNN model inaccuracies or external adversarial conditions. Therefore, this framework enables real-time verification of control inputs, optimizing for the closest safe control by maintaining adherence to safety constraints. Moreover, to enhance the systemâ€™s resilience, we incorporate protection against Denial of Service (DoS) attacks when deriving the safety conditions. The effectiveness of the developed framework is investigated in a high-fidelity simulation environment, CARLA, showcasing its resilience in dynamic and adversarial scenarios.
