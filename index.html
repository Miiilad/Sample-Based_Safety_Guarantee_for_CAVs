Sample-Based Safety Guarantee in Distributed Heterogeneous Vehicular Platooning for Deep Neural Network-Based Control

Milad Farsi1, Shuhao Bian,1, Nasser L. Azad1, Chris Hobbs2,
1Automation and Intelligent Systems Group, University of Waterloo, 2BlackBerry QNX

Abstract

This paper presents a novel approach for ensuring safety in distributed heterogeneous platooning systems using learning-based methods. The core contribution is the development of sample-based safety guarantees utilizing control barrier functions, which act as a shield to ensure safe operation even under uncertain conditions arising from neural network inaccuracies. This safety framework is integrated with a Model Predictive Control (MPC) scheme based on a deep neural network (DNN) model tailored for heterogeneous platooning. To enhance the systemâ€™s resilience, we incorporate protection against Denial-of-Service (DoS) attacks when deriving the safety conditions. Our approach enables real-time verification of control inputs, optimizing for the closest safe control while maintaining adherence to safety constraints. The effectiveness of this technique is demonstrated in a high-fidelity simulation environment, CARLA, showcasing its robustness in dynamic and adversarial scenarios.
